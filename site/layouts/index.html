Who built this — and why

I'm K. Shivaramakrishnan — a tissue-culture lab owner, plant seller, collector, breeder, and cultivar enthusiast. When I tried to build a proper online store for my plant business, every platform I touched was built for selling electronics or apparel. None of them understood TC batch provenance, seasonal shipping windows, phytosanitary requirements, or why a buyer needs to know a plant's exact growth stage before committing.

I spent months bolting things together with spreadsheets, manual DMs, and workarounds. Eventually I decided to build the tooling I wished existed — modules that make an e-commerce platform actually speak plant. PhytoCommerce is that tooling, open-sourced so no other plant business owner has to go through the same frustration.

— K. Shivaramakrishnan · LinkedIn · Forest Studio Labs

What Is PhytoCommerce?

Generic e-commerce platforms were built to sell shoes and electronics. Plant businesses are different. You track TC batch provenance. You manage seasonal availability and live arrival windows. You deal with phytosanitary certificates, KYC checks for wholesale pricing, and customers who need to know exactly what growth stage a plant is at before they commit.

PhytoCommerce is a suite of modules — 28 and counting — that slots into the platforms your developers already know, and gives your plant store the specialist features it actually needs. Any plant. Any scale. Any platform. No workarounds, no spreadsheets alongside your cart — just a store that speaks plant.

"We are specialty module developers — not tied to any single platform. We build the plant-commerce layer; you choose the foundation."

Live on PrestaShop 8 (28 modules, production-ready) and actively porting to WooCommerce. OpenCart, BigCommerce, and Magento Open Source are next — same feature set, idiomatic code per platform.
